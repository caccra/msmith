Understanding Insurance Policies in Uganda

Insurance contracts in Uganda are governed primarily by the Insurance Act (Cap 213) and the common law of contract. Every insurance policy is a legally binding agreement, and understanding its terms, particularly exclusions, conditions precedent to liability, and claims notification requirements, is critical before a dispute arises. Many claims are denied not because the loss is not covered in principle, but because the policyholder failed to comply with a procedural condition such as notifying the insurer within a specified period or providing supporting documentation in the required format.

The Insurance Regulatory Authority of Uganda (IRA), established under the Insurance Act, supervises all licensed insurers, reinsurers, brokers, and loss adjusters operating in Uganda. The IRA sets minimum capital requirements, product standards, and a market conduct framework that protects policyholders. Where an insurer engages in unfair claims practices, the IRA can be approached as a regulator, and where a formal complaint is warranted, we manage that process on your behalf alongside any parallel legal proceedings.

Common policy types in Uganda's market include fire and allied perils, motor, marine cargo, employer's liability, professional indemnity, directors and officers liability, group life, and medical insurance. Each product class has distinct legal and regulatory considerations. We advise businesses on selecting appropriate cover, reviewing policy wording before renewal, and ensuring that in the event of a claim, every procedural step is correctly followed to protect recovery rights.

How Insurance Disputes Are Resolved in Uganda

When an insurer wrongfully denies, delays, or underpays a legitimate claim in Uganda, several avenues for dispute resolution are available. The first step is usually a formal written complaint to the insurer's complaints department, demanding a reconsideration of the decision with reference to the specific policy provisions that support the claim. If internal escalation fails, a complaint can be filed with the Insurance Regulatory Authority of Uganda, which has powers to investigate, direct settlement, and impose regulatory action on non-compliant insurers.

Beyond regulatory complaints, policyholders can pursue civil litigation in the High Court of Uganda or, for smaller claims, the Magistrate's Court. Insurance disputes involving large commercial sums often also include arbitration clauses in the policy, particularly for reinsurance arrangements. Where a dispute involves cross-border insurance, for example, a policy underwritten in Kenya covering assets in Uganda, the choice of law and jurisdiction becomes an important consideration. We advise on the most strategically effective forum given the size, nature, and urgency of each dispute.

Subrogation rights are another frequently contested area. After paying a claim, an insurer may seek to recover that amount from the third party responsible for the loss. We represent both insurers pursuing subrogation actions and policyholders who need to understand the limits of their obligations to cooperate with such recovery efforts. Our goal is always to achieve the most commercially sensible outcome, whether through negotiated settlement or, where necessary, full litigation.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What should I do if my insurance claim is denied in Uganda?

    First, request a written explanation of the denial from your insurer. Then seek legal advice on whether the denial is justified under the policy wording. Many denials can be successfully challenged through internal complaints, Insurance Regulatory Authority complaints, or litigation. We review denied claims and advise on the strongest course of action.

  • Are insurance companies in Uganda regulated?

    Yes. The Insurance Regulatory Authority of Uganda (IRA) supervises all insurers, reinsurers, brokers, and agents operating in Uganda under the Insurance Act. The IRA also has a complaints mechanism for consumers. We advise clients on making regulatory complaints and can represent them in IRA proceedings.

  • What is subrogation in insurance law?

    Subrogation is the right of an insurer, after paying a claim, to step into the shoes of the insured and recover the amount paid from the party responsible for the loss. We advise insurers on pursuing subrogation actions and help policyholders understand their obligations when their insurer asserts subrogation rights.
