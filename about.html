Who We Are

One of the best law firms in Uganda — trusted by businesses, investors, and individuals across Kampala and East Africa who need more than legal advice. They need results that hold.

Our History

Built to Fill a Gap That Mattered

When Masari Aim Smith established M-Smith Advocates in Kampala in 2013, his starting conviction was straightforward: too many Ugandans and East African businesses were facing complex legal challenges without skilled, accessible, and genuinely committed representation. He built this firm to change that.

Over more than a decade, that conviction has shaped every client relationship we have formed. We have represented individual property buyers navigating Uganda's land fraud risks, entrepreneurs structuring their first companies, families caught in estate disputes, and multinational corporations managing cross-border regulatory complexity. In every case, we approach the matter the same way — as if the outcome is the only outcome that matters. Because to our clients, it is.

Today, M-Smith Advocates is recognised as one of the best law firms in Uganda, operating across 11 distinct practice areas — from Real Estate and Corporate Law to EAC Law, Oil & Gas, and Human Rights. We are one of the few law firms in Uganda capable of serving as a genuine full-service legal partner for the most complex, multi-disciplinary mandates across Kampala and the wider East African Community.

Regulated & Compliant

Professional Standards & Certifications

M-Smith Advocates operates under the full weight of Uganda's professional and regulatory framework. Our practice is enrolled, accredited, and held accountable to every body that governs legal practice in Uganda and East Africa.

Uganda Law Council — Enrolled Advocates
All practicing advocates at M-Smith Advocates are duly enrolled on the Roll of Advocates maintained by the Uganda Law Council under the Advocates Act (Cap 267). Enrollment confirms that each advocate has met the academic, professional training, and character requirements set by the Council.
Advocates Act Cap 267
Uganda Law Society — Active Membership
The firm and its advocates hold active membership with the Uganda Law Society (ULS) and are subject to its Code of Conduct and professional discipline. ULS membership is a mark of good professional standing and a commitment to ethical legal practice across Uganda.
ULS Member in Good Standing
East African Law Society — Regional Accreditation
Our advocates hold membership with the East African Law Society (EALS), the premier regional bar body serving all EAC member states. This accreditation underpins our authority to advise on cross-border EAC matters and appear before regional courts and tribunals including the East African Court of Justice.
EALS & EACJ Accredited
Anti-Money Laundering & Counter-Terrorism Financing
M-Smith Advocates is fully compliant with Uganda's Anti-Money Laundering Act 2013 (as amended) and the obligations of the Financial Intelligence Authority (FIA). We conduct mandatory client due diligence (CDD) and Know Your Customer (KYC) checks on all new matters, and maintain suspicious transaction reporting procedures as required by law.
AML Act 2013 • FIA Compliant
Data Protection & Privacy Act 2019
All client personal data is handled in full compliance with Uganda's Data Protection and Privacy Act 2019. We collect only what is necessary, store it securely, and never share or sell personal information without lawful authority. Clients have the right to access, correct, and request deletion of their personal data held by the firm.
DPPA 2019 Compliant
Advocate-Client Privilege & Professional Conduct Rules
Every communication between a client and M-Smith Advocates is protected by full legal professional privilege under the Evidence Act (Cap 6) and the Advocates Act. The firm is additionally bound by the Advocates (Professional Conduct) Regulations, enforced by the Advocates Disciplinary Committee — ensuring our conduct is not only ethical but enforceable.
Evidence Act Cap 6 • Professional Conduct Regulations
