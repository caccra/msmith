Selected Matters

A representative sample of the work our lawyers in Uganda and Kampala have done for clients across East Africa. Client identities are kept confidential in accordance with our professional obligations.

Real Estate & Land
Fraudulent Title Uncovered — Shs 2.4 Billion Property Transaction Saved
ClientForeign Private Investor
JurisdictionKampala, Uganda
Our client was on the verge of completing a commercial property purchase in Kampala. During due diligence, our team identified irregularities in the title history at the Land Registry. A deeper investigation revealed the seller had no lawful ownership — the title had been fraudulently transferred from the registered proprietor. We advised the client to immediately withdraw from the transaction and notified the relevant authorities. The client retained their full investment and was connected to a legitimate property through our network.
Outcome: Full investment protected. Fraud reported. Clean alternative transaction completed.
Constitutional Law & Human Rights
Constitutional Petition — Enforcement of Fundamental Rights Under Article 50
CourtHigh Court of Uganda (Civil Division)
CitationMisc. Cause 277/2018
M-Smith Advocates appeared in a constitutional petition before the High Court of Uganda Civil Division, filed under Article 50(1) and (4) of the Constitution of Uganda and the Judicature (Fundamental Rights and Freedoms) (Enforcement Procedure) Rules. The firm filed a supporting affidavit in proceedings against the Attorney General, seeking enforcement of the petitioner's fundamental rights. The matter demonstrates the firm's readiness to engage at the highest levels of Uganda's constitutional framework.
Outcome: Constitutional petition filed and argued before the High Court of Uganda.
Corporate & Immigration
Multinational Market Entry — Company Registration, Permits & Regulatory Clearance
ClientEast African FMCG Company
JurisdictionUganda (EAC Cross-border)
A regional FMCG business entering the Ugandan market engaged us to manage the full legal establishment process. We handled company incorporation at URSB, drafted shareholder and director agreements, obtained trading licences, and secured work permits for seven expatriate staff within a compressed timeline. We also advised on national content requirements and ensured the client's operations were structured to qualify for preferential EAC treatment under the Common Market Protocol.
Outcome: Full market entry completed in under 60 days. All 7 work permits approved first attempt.
Employment & Labour
Employer Defence — Wrongful Termination Claim Reduced by 80%
ClientUgandan SME (Manufacturing Sector)
ForumIndustrial Court of Uganda
A manufacturing client faced a wrongful termination claim from a former senior employee demanding 18 months' salary in compensation plus reinstatement. The claimant alleged procedural irregularities in the dismissal process. Our team reviewed the full employment file, demonstrated that fair procedure had been followed under the Employment Act 2006, and challenged the quantum of the claim. Through strategic pre-trial negotiations backed by a strong legal position, we secured a settlement at a fraction of the original claim — avoiding a lengthy industrial court hearing.
Outcome: Claim settled at 20% of original demand. Reinstatement request dismissed entirely.
Intellectual Property
Trademark Portfolio Protection — Infringement Stopped & Damages Recovered
ClientUgandan Consumer Brand
JurisdictionUganda & Kenya
A client discovered a competitor was selling counterfeit goods under a confusingly similar brand name across Uganda and Kenya. The infringer had not registered the mark but was using it in trade dress and packaging. We filed for an emergency injunction in Uganda, coordinated with Kenyan counsel for parallel proceedings, and secured the removal of infringing products from distribution. We also negotiated a settlement that included a damages payment and formal undertaking to cease use of the mark permanently.
Outcome: Injunction granted. Infringing products removed. Damages recovered across two jurisdictions.
Litigation & Debt Recovery
Commercial Debt Recovery — UGX 680M Recovered on Disputed Contract
ClientConstruction Contractor
CourtCommercial Court of Uganda
A construction contractor was owed UGX 680 million by a developer who disputed the final payment milestone on a completed project. The developer claimed defects in workmanship as grounds for non-payment. We filed in the Commercial Court of Uganda, secured an interim order preserving the developer's assets pending judgment, and appointed an independent expert to verify completion standards. The expert report vindicated our client. The developer settled in full before trial to avoid a court ruling and the associated costs award.
Outcome: Full UGX 680M recovered. Settlement reached pre-trial. Costs awarded to client.
