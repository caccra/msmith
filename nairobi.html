Why Our Nairobi Office

Uganda–Kenya Legal Corridor

Businesses operating across the Uganda–Kenya border face a dual regulatory environment, two company law regimes, two immigration systems, and two sets of employment obligations. M-Smith Advocates is one of the few firms with a genuine physical presence in both countries, eliminating the coordination overhead of managing separate counsel in each jurisdiction.

Our Nairobi office provides direct access to Kenya-qualified professionals who work within the same firm as our Kampala-based advocates. For multinationals, NGOs, and EAC investors, this means consistent legal strategy across your entire East African footprint, from a single relationship, with one point of contact.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Does M-Smith Advocates handle Kenyan law matters from Nairobi?

    Yes. Our Nairobi office handles corporate law, company registration, immigration, employment, commercial litigation, and EAC cross-border matters under Kenyan law. We work closely with the Kampala headquarters on cross-border Uganda–Kenya transactions.

  • Is M-Smith Advocates accredited to practise in Kenya?

    Our Kenya-based team operates in compliance with the Advocates Act (Kenya) and the Law Society of Kenya regulations. We advise on Kenyan law matters and coordinate with enrolled LSK advocates for matters requiring local court representation.

  • What EAC legal services does the Nairobi office provide?

    Our Nairobi office specialises in EAC Common Market Protocol compliance, cross-border trade facilitation, regional arbitration, and EACJ proceedings. We assist businesses entering Kenya from Uganda and other EAC states with regulatory setup, work permits, and corporate structuring.
